Project Meetings

The Seam project meets every week online on IRC to cover a mix of technical architecture and project planning. If you are interested in working on a Seam module, or just want to know more about the progress of Seam 3, this is a great opportunity to drop by!

This page contains Hack Night information, meeting information, agendas and links to transcripts for these meetings.

Seam Hack Night

The Seam Hack Night is a time where all those interested in Seam 3 can get together and hack on a given module. Each month on the second Thursday from 10:00 - 03:00 AM UTC community members gather in IRC to discuss and hack on the designated Seam module for the night. The lead of the module will post a list of JIRA tickets to be worked on ranging from simple to complex, so everyone is invited to participate!

Meeting information

The Seam project meetings are held in the open in the #seam-dev channel on the Freenode IRC network. Everyone is welcome to attend.

Here are the meeting details:

  • Topics: A mix of technical architecture and project planning
  • IRC Server: irc.freenode.net
  • Channel: #seam-dev (transcripts available)
  • Date/Time: 21:00 UTC (see times in example date) every Wednesday (~45 minutes)
    Winter: 07:00 AM Brisbane (Thr), 16:00 New York (Wed), 14:00 Denver (Wed)
    Summer: 07:00 AM Brisbane (Thu), 17:00 New York (Wed), 15:00 Denver (Wed)
Office hour(s) for module leads to ask questions and work through issues begins immediately following the meeting.

See the Chat page for more information about how to setup your IRC client and joining the Seam-related channels.

If you can't make the meeting, you can read the meeting transcripts below. You can also chat with us informally on #seam-dev or one of the other IRC channels where we hang out during the week.

Agenda

If agenda items are posted before the meeting, we will attempt to follow them to keep the meeting moving and to avoid overlooking issues. However, some meetings may be conducted in a more free-form manner in order to collect status updates across the project.
